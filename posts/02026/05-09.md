# Potato and Onion Planting
Today with the threat of freezing receding, I first prepped the Southwest
bed by creating four rows running East-to-West:

![Southwest Bed with four rows](img/02026-05-09-sw-bed-rows.png)

For the North-most row, I planted Russet Potatoes, the next row
was a mixture of red, yellow, and white onions, followed by a
row of Red Potatoes, with the South-most bed planted with the 
remaining onions. 

Here is the bed with the potato and onion bulbs with a photo-bomb
by Cannon:

![Southwest Bed with Cannon](img/02026-05-09-sw-bed-rows-and-cannon.png)

After finishing the planting, I gave the full bed a good soaking of 
water. I need to water regularly for the rest of the summer.

![Southwest Bed Watered](img/02026-05-09-sw-bed-watered-rows.png)

I think of this planting as an ode to my European peasant ancestors
as the survival garden. If I have a good harvest, I hope to have at 
least 15 kilograms of potatoes and 10-15 kilograms of onions. Such 
a harvest would be immediately valued and provide the necessary calories
for a long cold winter in Scandinavia.
