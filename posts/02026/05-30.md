# Replanting Seedlings, Adding a Small Piece to the Semantic Web, and Repurposing Existing Tools
Whew! A long title but I did a lot today in the garden. First, many of the
peppers, cucumbers, and tomato plants died in southwest bed so yesterday I
went to [Bayfield Gardens](https://bayfieldgardens.com/) and purchased $36 worth of tomato, pepper,
and cucumber seedlings. When I was checking I asked why the plants I purchased
earlier in the month, died. She suggested that the pepper plants were getting 
overwatered and the early May cool temperatures killed off the cucumbers and
tomatos.

## Repurposing Existing Tools
As a frugal person, I like to reuse or repurpose existing tools as much as I can and
recycle what would end-up in a landfill. All of the garden beds contain many rocks
and small pebbles, so I used an old paint strainer to sift the dirt to remove the 
bigger rocks from the soil before planting. Here is a photo of the paint strainer
repurposed (with my bare foot for good measure :-):

![Paint can repurposed](img/02026-05-30-paint-strainer-as-soil-sifter.png)

Finally, I recycled a couple of cardboard egg containers to start some corn, zucchini,
and pumpkins plants from seeds using the cardboard box that was used to transport 
the seedlings:

![Corn, Zucchini, and Pumpkin seeds](img/02026-05-30-egg-carton-seeds.png)
